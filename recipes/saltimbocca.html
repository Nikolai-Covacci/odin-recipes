Saltimbocca

Description

Saltimbocca is a classic Italian dish made with thin slices of veal topped with prosciutto and sage, pan-fried, and finished in a white wine and butter sauce. The name means “jumps in the mouth,” a nod to its bold, savory flavor. It’s traditionally Roman and known for its quick cooking and rich, salty bite.

This recipe stays true to the Roman tradition by using veal, but emphasizes ease and speed, taking less than 10 minutes to cook. The veal is lightly floured before being pan-seared, which helps create a golden crust and slightly thickens the sauce. Sage and prosciutto are secured with toothpicks, adding both flavor and structure, while a splash of wine and butter creates a simple yet elegant finish.

Ingredients

  • 4 Thin Veal Fillets
  • 4 Thin slices of Prosciutto
  • 1 Bunch of Sage
  • 3.4 tbsp oz of Butter
  • 1/2 cup of Dry White Wine
  • 1 tbsp Extra Virgin Olive Oil
  • 1/4 cup Flour
  • 4-8 Toothpicks
  • Salt and Pepper as needed

Recipe Steps

  1. Use a meat mallet to thin the veal cutlets.
  2. Lay a slice of prosciutto on top of each cutlet.
  3. Add a sage leaf in the middle of the prosciutto, using a toothpick to hold it in place.
  4. Lightly cover each cutlet with flour on the side without prosciutto.
  5. Heat a large frying pan over medium-low heat with your olive oil and butter.
  6. Add extra sage to the pan for flavor.
  7. Without overcrowding your pan add the veal slices into the pan with the prosciutto side facing up.
  8. Cook without flipping until the veal is brown and the prosciutto is crisp.
  9. Carefully add white wine to the pan and cook until the alcohol evaporates and the sauce thickens.
  10. Add Salt and Pepper if needed.
  11. Remove toothpicks, spoon the sauce over the veal, and serve immediately.

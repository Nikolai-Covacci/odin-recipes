Penne all'Arrabbiata

Description

Penne all'Arrabbiata is a classic Roman pasta dish known for its bold, spicy flavor. It features penne pasta tossed in a

simple yet fiery tomato sauce made with garlic, olive oil, and plenty of red chili peppers. The name "arrabbiata" means

"angry" in Italian, referring to the heat of the dish. The sauce is cooked until rich and slightly thick, then mixed with

al dente penne to create a flavorful, satisfying meal. It's often garnished with fresh parsley and sometimes a sprinkle of

Pecorino Romano cheese for added sharpness, making it a favorite among lovers of spicy Italian food.

Ingredients

  • 12 oz of Penne rigate pasta
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 lb of fresh tomato pulp
  • 3 dried hot chili peppers
  • 4 tbsp of extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp of fresh chopped parsley
  • salt, to taste
  • Grated cheese

Recipe Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook 12 oz of penne rigate until al dente.
  2. While the pasta cooks, heat 4 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil in a pan over low heat.
  3. Add 1 peeled clove of garlic and 3 dried hot chili peppers to the oil and sauté for 2-3 minutes, being careful not to burn the garlic.
  4. Add 1 pound of fresh tomato pulp to the pan, season with salt to taste, and simmer the sauce on low heat for about 10 minutes.
  5. Remove the garlic clove once the sauce is cooked and flavorful.
  6. Drain the pasta and add it directly to the pan with the sauce, tossing to coat evenly.
  7. Stir in 2 tablespoons of freshly chopped parsley and mix well.
  8. Serve hot, topped with grated cheese if desired.
